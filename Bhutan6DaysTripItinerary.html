Bhutan 6 Days Trip

About The Place

A small, landlocked country nestled deep in the Himalayas between India and China, Bhutan is characterized by steep mountains and deep valleys, which led to scattered population settlement patterns. The country is famous for its unique philosophy – Gross National Happiness.

Itinerary

  • Day 1 : Kathmandu/Bangkok ✈ Paro-Thimpu

    Flight Hotel
    • Highlights：Takin Zoo, National Monument, Tashi Quezong
    • Take an international flight to Bhutan and arrive at Paro Airport, the only international airport in the area.
    • When you get off the plane, you can see large photos of the king and the princess, as if you are welcoming guests from afar. The Bhutanese tour guide presents a white hada and sincerely wish you all success!
    • Drive to the capital of Bhutan-Thimphu。Along the way, we saw green mountains, clear rivers, and breathed fresh air. In the next trip, we will gradually experience the artistic conception of the benevolent and the wise！
    • Visit【Takin Zoo】，See Tajin, the national beast of Bhutan. It is a statue of a cow with a sheep face. Bhutan regards it as an auspicious national beast.
    • The iconic building of Thimphu -- Bhutan【National monument】。To commemorate His Majesty King Jigme Dorje Wangchuck III (Father of Modern Bhutan)， 1974 Completed in the year, it is also to pray for peace in the world. This is the most concentrated resort for the people of Thimphu who pass through the tower every day。The locals believe that the turret can wash away the sins committed earlier. This is also a pagoda, with many sacred Tantric Buddha statues and bi-paintings inside.
    • Visit after 5pm【Tashi Kezong】，This is the "Zhongnanhai" of Bhutan. It was built in the 13th century and stands 2500 meters above sea level. It stands solemnly on the west bank of Wangchu River。If you are lucky, you can also enjoy the flag-lowering ceremony held. After dinner, free time in Thimphu city.
    • Meals included: Dinner (Chinese food according to flight arrangement)
    • Overnight stay at Thimpu Class A Hotel

  • Day 2 : Second Day At Thimpu

    Hotel
    • Highlights: Open-air Sakyamuni Buddha Statue, National Academy of Traditional Arts, Bhutan State Post Office, Bhutan Paper Factory.
    • After breakfast, go to the observation deck on the outskirts of the city to get a bird’s eye view of Thimphu’s cityscape and the scenery of the valley.【Statue of Sakyamuni Buddha in the open air】，Standing 51 meters high, standing under the solemn Buddha statue, there is a shocking feeling, as if receiving the blessing of Buddha's mercy and wisdom, and gradually feel how the spirit of Buddhism has penetrated into the hearts of the people in this country.
    • Visit【National Academy of Traditional Arts】，Here you can learn 13 kinds of traditional crafts and provide students with 6 years of comprehensive and systematic education.
    • Visit【Bhutan National Post Office】，Bhutan is the most famous "stamp country" in the world. The first stamp that humans can sing was born in Bhutan in 1962. Here, you can see stamps of various materials：Paper, silk, plastic, metal, etc. Even the themes of stamps are very rich。Here you only need to provide the electronic version of the photos to make personal head stamps on the spot. These "DIY stamps" are even equivalent to the state-issued stamps and can also be used for mailing and preservation.
    • Visit【Bhutan Paper Mill】Learn how Bhutanese use simple traditional techniques to produce high-quality paper that can be stored for a long time.
    • Meals included: Breakfast, Lunch and Dinner
    • Overnight stay at Thimpu Class A Hotel

  • Day 3 : Thimpu - Punaka

    Hotel
    • Highlights: Sightseeing at Dochula Pass, Chukwangye Monument, Punakha Dzong, Chemilakon.
    • After breakfast, after an hour’s drive, we will arrive【Dochula pass】，It is 3140 meters above sea level and is one of the most beautiful observation decks in Bhutan.
    • We can enjoy the magnificent view of the Himalayas while drinking coffee here。Here you can also take a closer look at the epic lustre【108 stupas】，is also called【Chukwangye Monument】，It was built on December 6, 2003 by the Fourth Queen Mother of Bhutan, Dorje Wangm Wangchuck, to pray for the safe return of the militant groups in the northeastern part of India in southern Bhutan from the king and soldiers of Bhutan。Nowadays, colorful prayer flags are hung here, and it is a holy place for local people to pray for the blessings of gods.
    • Visit one of the most beautiful Dzong Forts in Bhutan ---【Punakha Dzong】。Before 1950, Punakha was the national capital of Bhutan and the political center and seat of the national government of Bhutan。Construction began in 1637。This vicissitudes of life fortress and the great father of Bhutan: Xia Zun Ngawang Namgyal has a special fate. Knowing his brief history is tantamount to understanding an important piece of Bhutan’s history.
    • Visit the high hills by the river【Chemilacan】，The temple built by the cousin of the lama Drukpa Kunlie to commemorate his surrender of the devil. Drukpa Kunlie is a saint widely revered by the Bhutanese，His method was very different from the social customs of the time, but he revealed to people the true meaning of Buddhism. The people listened to him as if they were watching humorous drama sketches. They laughed forward and backward, and called them crazy holy monks。This place is a holy place for child-seeking. After many years of childless believers who have been blessed by a lama here, they will be pregnant when they return.
    • Meals included: Breakfast, Lunch and Dinner
    • Overnight stay at Plaka Class A Hotel

  • Day 4 : Plaka - Thimpu - Paro

    Hotel
    • Highlights: Kamshayelinaye Monument, Paro Dzong, local song and dance performances and food experience.
    • Visit after breakfast【Kam Shayeri Naye Monument】This 30-meter-high pagoda took 6 years to build. The pagoda is designed to pray for the fifth king to protect the country. Each floor enshrines different guardian gods.
    • Go to【Parozong】It is also called "Ripeng Fort" in the sense of the pile of jewels on the fort. It is the most famous Dzong Fort in Bhutan. It was built in 1644. It is also the most outstanding representative of Bhutan's architectural art. The famous Bhutanese movie "Little Living Buddha" was shot here.
    • Bhutan is the only organic crop kingdom in the world. If we have time, we can visit Bhutan's food markets to observe the living and diet of Bhutanese from one side, and see what is the connection between diet and happiness! Enjoy local folk song and dance performances and experience local specialties during dinner.
    • Meals included: Breakfast, Lunch and Dinner
    • Overnight stay at Paro Class A Hotel

  • Day 5 - Paro

    Hotel
    • Essence attractions: Tiger Cave Temple, Qichu Temple.
    • After a few days’ journey, Bhutanese happiness seems to be getting closer and closer to us. Today we are going to feel that happiness is neither far away nor unreachable, nor is it a gift that can be obtained at will！
    • Depart for the most sacred in Bhutan【Tiger Cave Temple】，Known as one of the top ten temples in the world, it is a holy place for Padmasambhava. It is built on the cliffs of the canyon over 900 meters. You need to walk more than 2 hours to go up the mountain. People who are not used to sports may find it a little difficult；But as long as your heart returns to the present, you will find that the road to happiness may be a bit like climbing a tiger's cave temple! I hope you not only enjoy the beautiful scenery, but also get the wonderful experience when you reach the top！
    • Go down the mountain to one of the oldest and most beautiful temples in Bhutan ——【Qichu Temple】Visit, this temple was ordered by the Tibetan king Songtsen Gampo in 659 to build. The great Dilgo Khyentse Rinpoche once presided over this temple. The eight-year-old Sakyamuni is enshrined in the temple as one of the most sacred Buddha statues in Bhutan. 1. It was built in the same era as the Potala Palace in Tibet and is equally outstanding.
    • In the afternoon, men put on silk, women put on chira, and head to Uma/Amman Hotel to enjoy a leisurely and pleasant afternoon tea time.
    • Meals included: Breakfast, Lunch and Dinner
    • Overnight stay at Paro Class A Hotel

  • Day 6 - Paro ✈ Kathmandu/Bangkok

    Flight
    • A staff member will drop you off to the airport and bid farewell.
    • The wonderful trip is over.
    • Meals included: Breakfast
